Pro Tips

Resume Tips That Get You Shortlisted

Actionable advice from recruiters and hiring managers. Follow these tips to make your resume stand out from hundreds of applicants.

1

Tailor Your Resume for Every Job

A generic resume gets ignored. Study the job description carefully and mirror the exact keywords, skills, and phrases the employer uses. ATS systems scan for keyword matches, so aligning your resume with each JD dramatically improves your chances.

Use LevelUpX's "Analyze Resume Against JD" feature to instantly see which keywords you're missing and get a tailored rewrite.

2

Lead With Impact, Not Duties

Recruiters spend 6-7 seconds scanning a resume. Replace vague duty descriptions with quantified achievements. Use the formula: Action Verb + What You Did + Measurable Result.

Before

Responsible for managing social media accounts

After

Grew Instagram following from 5K to 50K in 8 months, increasing lead generation by 35%

3

Keep It Clean: One Page, Clear Sections

For most professionals with under 10 years of experience, stick to one page. Use clear section headers (Summary, Experience, Skills, Education) and consistent formatting. White space is your friend — cluttered resumes get rejected.

Simple fonts (Arial, Calibri) 10-12pt size 0.5-1 inch margins No photos or graphics
4

Write a Powerful Professional Summary

Your summary is prime real estate. In 2-3 lines, state who you are, your key expertise, and the value you bring. Avoid cliches like "hard-working team player." Be specific about your domain, years of experience, and standout achievements.

Example

"Senior Product Manager with 6+ years driving B2B SaaS growth. Led a cross-functional team of 12 to launch a platform that generated $4M ARR in its first year."

5

Use Strong Action Verbs

Start every bullet point with a power verb. Avoid weak starters like "Helped," "Assisted," or "Was responsible for." Strong verbs show ownership and leadership.

Spearheaded Orchestrated Delivered Accelerated Optimized Architected Transformed Pioneered
6

Optimize for ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)

Over 75% of companies use ATS to filter resumes before a human ever sees them. Use standard section headings, avoid tables/columns/headers/footers, and save as PDF. Never use images, icons, or fancy formatting that ATS can't parse.

COMMON ATS KILLERS

Two-column layouts Graphics & icons Headers & footers Text boxes Unusual fonts
7

Include a Skills Section With Keywords

Add a dedicated Skills section near the top of your resume. List technical skills, tools, certifications, and methodologies relevant to the job. This helps both ATS and recruiters quickly assess your fit.

EXAMPLE SKILLS SECTION

Technical: Python, SQL, Tableau, AWS, Docker, CI/CD

Frameworks: React, Node.js, Django, TensorFlow

Certifications: AWS Solutions Architect, PMP, Google Analytics

8

Proofread Ruthlessly — Zero Typos Allowed

A single typo can get your resume rejected. It signals carelessness. Read your resume backwards (sentence by sentence) to catch errors. Use tools like Grammarly, and always have someone else review it before submitting.

CHECKLIST

  • Consistent date formatting (Month Year)
  • No spelling or grammar errors
  • Consistent bullet style and tense
  • Working email and phone number
